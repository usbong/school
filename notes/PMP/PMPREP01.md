> Explanation: The Agile Suitability Filters Questionnaire is a tool used to help teams identify potential roadblocks and risks to agile adoption. It is not intended to provide a step-by-step guide or identify a specific agile methodology. Instead, it helps teams determine if their project is suitable for an agile approach and identify potential challenges that may need to be addressed.

> Explanation: There are four ceremonies (meetings) as a part of the agile practice:

> Retrospectives: The single most important practice because it allows the team to learn about, improve, and adapt it’s process

> Daily Standup: Teams use standups to microcommit to each other, uncover problems, and ensure the work flows smoothly through the team.

> Demonstrations/Reviews: As the team completes the features, the team periodically demonstrates the working product. The product owner sees the demonstration and accepts or declines the user stories.

> Iteration (Sprint) Planning: The team meets with the product owner to consider story size and make a commitment to what can be completing within an iteration (sprint). Agile teams do not plan just once in one single chunk but rather plan a little, deliver, learn, and then replan more into the next cycle.
